05. The Giant King

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets combined.

The King of Planets

Jupiter is composed primarily of hydrogen, but a quarter of its mass is helium, although helium comprises only about a tenth of the number of molecules. It may also have a rocky core of heavier elements, but like the other giant planets, Jupiter lacks a well-defined solid surface. The outer atmosphere is visibly segregated into several bands at different latitudes, resulting in turbulence and storms along their interacting boundaries. A prominent result is the Great Red Spot, a giant storm that is known to have existed since at least the 17th century when it was first seen by telescope. Surrounding Jupiter is a faint planetary ring system and a powerful magnetosphere. Jupiter has 95 known moons and possibly many more, including the four large Galilean moons discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610. Ganymede, the largest of these, has a diameter greater than that of the planet Mercury.

Jupiter's immense gravity acts as a shield for the inner solar system, sweeping up or deflecting asteroids and comets that might otherwise impact Earth. However, it also has the power to fling objects towards us. Its influence is so great that it shapes the structure of the entire asteroid belt.

The Galileo mission (1989-2003) provided us with incredible insights into the Jovian system. It dropped a probe into Jupiter's atmosphere, which survived for about an hour before being crushed by the immense pressure. The probe measured winds of up to 400 miles per hour and found less water than expected. The Juno mission, currently orbiting Jupiter, is peering deep into the planet's heart to determine if it has a solid core and to map its intense magnetic field.

(Content Extension...) The Galilean moons are worlds in their own right. Io is the most volcanically active body in the solar system, with plumes of sulfur rising hundreds of miles into space. Europa is an icy world with a subsurface ocean that contains more water than all of Earth's oceans combined; it is one of the most promising places to search for life beyond Earth. Ganymede is the only moon known to have its own magnetic field. Callisto, the most heavily cratered object in the solar system, is a frozen remnant of the early solar system.

Jupiter radiates more heat than it receives from the Sun. This internal heat source drives the turbulence in its atmosphere. If Jupiter had been about 75 times more massive, it would have ignited nuclear fusion and become a star. Thus, it is often referred to as a "failed star," though this is a bit of a misnomer as it formed very differently from a star.

The Great Red Spot is shrinking. Once large enough to fit three Earths, it is now only about the width of one. Scientists are unsure if it will disappear entirely or if it is just part of a long-term cycle. The storm is essentially a high-pressure anticyclone that has been raging for hundreds of years.
